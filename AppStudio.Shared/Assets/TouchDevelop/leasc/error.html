About Browser Storage

TouchDevelop stores your scripts and data in a database maintained by your browser. Browsers impose restrictions on whether a website is allowed to store data, and how much data it may use. Go to the settings of your browser and make sure that touchdevelop.com is allowed to store (more) data.

For example, in the desktop version of Internet Explorer, click on the tools wheel in the menu bar, select "Internet options". In the "General" tab, locate the "Browsing history" section.

  • Select "Settings", click on the "Caches and databases" tab. Make sure that "Allow website caches and databases" is checked, "touchdevelop.com" is listed, and is allowed to "Exceed limit".
  • If the previous suggestion didn't help, try to "Delete..." your "Browsing history", in particular select "Temporary Internet files and website files" and "Cookies and website data".

Internet Explorer

In the desktop version of Internet Explorer, click on the tools wheel in the menu bar, select "Internet options". In the "General" tab, locate the "Browsing history" section.

  • Select "Settings", click on the "Caches and databases" tab. Make sure that "Allow website caches and databases" is checked, "touchdevelop.com" is listed, and is allowed to "Exceed limit".
  • If the previous suggestion didn't help, try to "Delete..." your "Browsing history", in particular select "Temporary Internet files and website files" and "Cookies and website data".

Mobile Safari

  • In iOS 6, open "Settings", select "Safari", and turn "Private Browsing" off.
  • In iOS 7, open "Safari", tap on the Bookmarks button, then tap on the "Private" button in the lower left corner of the screen; if asked, select "Close All" pages.

Desktop Safari

  • In Safari, open "Preferences", select "Privacy", and turn "Block cookies and other website data" to "Never".
